Despite a decade of "human-centred" rhetoric, few speakers referenced customers, empathy, or design.
Design, once a bridge between creativity and strategy, has become linguistically invisible—too operational for boardroom gravity.

Without it, strategy risks abstraction: decisions detached from lived experience, metrics replacing meaning.
