Concept:

Baumol's Cost Disease is the invisible driver of value in human labour.

As technology multiplies efficiency in the abstract world, the real world becomes relatively expensive.

Electricians, teachers, nurses, and musicians share the same economic fate: they trade in irreplaceable attention and skill.

The price of embodiment rises with every algorithm that cannot touch a wire.
